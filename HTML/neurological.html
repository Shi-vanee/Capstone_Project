Neurological and Genetic Disorders

Neurological disorders affect the brain, spinal cord, and nerves, while genetic disorders are caused by abnormalities in an individual's DNA. Some conditions, like dementia and intellectual disability, can have both neurological and genetic components.

Dementia

A group of symptoms affecting memory, thinking and social abilities severely enough to interfere with daily functioning.

Dementia

Dementia is not a single disease but rather a term that describes a group of symptoms associated with a decline in memory or other thinking skills severe enough to reduce a person's ability to perform everyday activities. Alzheimer's disease accounts for 60-80% of cases.

Symptoms

  • Memory loss that disrupts daily life
  • Challenges in planning or solving problems
  • Difficulty completing familiar tasks
  • Confusion with time or place
  • Trouble understanding visual images and spatial relationships
  • New problems with words in speaking or writing
  • Misplacing things and losing the ability to retrace steps
  • Decreased or poor judgment
  • Withdrawal from work or social activities
  • Changes in mood and personality

Treatment and Management

  • Medications to temporarily improve symptoms
  • Cognitive stimulation therapy
  • Behavioral interventions
  • Creating a safe and supportive environment
  • Regular physical exercise
  • Social engagement and activities
  • Healthy diet (Mediterranean diet may help)
  • Management of other health conditions
  • Support for caregivers

Intellectual Disability

Intellectual disability involves limitations in intellectual functioning (such as learning, problem solving, judgment) and adaptive behavior (conceptual, social, and practical skills). These limitations affect functioning in conceptual, social, and practical areas of life.

Characteristics

  • Delayed developmental milestones (sitting, crawling, walking)
  • Delayed language development
  • Difficulty learning and problem solving
  • Problems with memory
  • Difficulty understanding social rules
  • Problems with adaptive behaviors like personal care
  • Difficulty seeing consequences of actions
  • Problems with planning and time management

Support and Intervention

  • Early intervention programs (birth to 3 years)
  • Special education and individualized education plans (IEPs)
  • Speech and language therapy
  • Occupational therapy
  • Behavioral therapy
  • Social skills training
  • Vocational training
  • Family counseling and support
  • Community support services

Other Neurological and Genetic Disorders

Autism Spectrum Disorder

A developmental disorder that affects communication and behavior, with symptoms typically appearing in the first two years of life.

Down Syndrome

A genetic chromosome 21 disorder causing developmental and intellectual delays and physical characteristics.

Fragile X Syndrome

A genetic condition causing intellectual disability, behavioral and learning challenges, and various physical characteristics.

Cerebral Palsy

A group of disorders affecting movement, muscle tone, and posture caused by damage to the developing brain.

Epilepsy

A central nervous system disorder in which brain activity becomes abnormal, causing seizures or periods of unusual behavior.

Huntington's Disease

An inherited condition that causes the progressive breakdown of nerve cells in the brain.
