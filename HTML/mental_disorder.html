Mental Disorders

Comprehensive information about common mental health conditions and their treatment

Anxiety Disorders

Types

  • Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD): Persistent and excessive worry
  • Panic Disorder: Recurrent panic attacks
  • Social Anxiety Disorder: Intense fear of social situations
  • Specific Phobias: Irrational fear of specific objects/situations
  • Agoraphobia: Fear of places/situations that might cause panic
  • Separation Anxiety Disorder: Excessive fear about separation from home/attachment figures

Causes

  • Biological factors: Brain chemistry, genetics
  • Environmental factors: Trauma, stress buildup
  • Medical factors: Side effects of medication, symptoms of illness
  • Personality: Certain personality types more prone
  • Substance use: Can cause or worsen anxiety

Treatment

  • Psychotherapy: CBT most effective
  • Medications: Antidepressants, anti-anxiety drugs, beta-blockers
  • Lifestyle changes: Exercise, stress management, sleep hygiene
  • Support groups: Sharing experiences and coping strategies
  • Relaxation techniques: Meditation, deep breathing, yoga

Personality Disorders

Cluster A (Odd, Eccentric)

  • Paranoid Personality Disorder: Pervasive distrust and suspicion
  • Schizoid Personality Disorder: Detachment from social relationships
  • Schizotypal Personality Disorder: Acute discomfort in close relationships

Cluster B (Dramatic, Emotional)

  • Antisocial Personality Disorder: Disregard for others' rights
  • Borderline Personality Disorder: Instability in relationships, self-image
  • Histrionic Personality Disorder: Excessive emotionality and attention-seeking
  • Narcissistic Personality Disorder: Grandiosity, need for admiration

Cluster C (Anxious, Fearful)

  • Avoidant Personality Disorder: Social inhibition, feelings of inadequacy
  • Dependent Personality Disorder: Excessive need to be cared for
  • Obsessive-Compulsive Personality Disorder: Preoccupation with orderliness

Causes

  • Genetics: Family history increases risk
  • Childhood trauma: Abuse, neglect, unstable family life
  • Verbal abuse: Childhood verbal abuse linked to certain disorders
  • Brain changes: Differences in brain structure and chemistry

Diagnosis

  • Psychological evaluation
  • Diagnostic criteria from DSM-5
  • Medical history review
  • Assessment of symptoms and impact on functioning

Treatment

  • Psychotherapy: DBT, CBT, psychodynamic therapy
  • Medications: Antidepressants, mood stabilizers, antipsychotics
  • Hospitalization: For severe cases or crises
  • Support groups: Peer support and coping strategies

Bipolar Disorder

Overview

Bipolar disorder causes extreme mood swings that include emotional highs (mania or hypomania) and lows (depression). These mood swings can affect sleep, energy, activity, judgment, behavior, and the ability to think clearly.

Types

  • Bipolar I Disorder: At least one manic episode
  • Bipolar II Disorder: At least one hypomanic and one depressive episode
  • Cyclothymic Disorder: Periods of hypomanic and depressive symptoms
  • Other Specified Bipolar Disorder: Symptoms don't match the above categories

Symptoms

Manic/Hypomanic Symptoms:

  • Abnormally upbeat or wired
  • Increased activity/energy
  • Exaggerated sense of well-being
  • Decreased need for sleep
  • Racing thoughts
  • Poor decision-making

Depressive Symptoms:

  • Depressed mood
  • Loss of interest in activities
  • Significant weight changes
  • Insomnia or sleeping too much
  • Fatigue or loss of energy
  • Feelings of worthlessness
  • Thoughts of death/suicide

Diagnosis

  • Physical exam
  • Psychiatric assessment
  • Mood charting
  • Comparison to DSM-5 criteria
  • Medical tests to rule out other conditions

Causes

  • Biological differences: Physical changes in the brain
  • Genetics: More common in people with first-degree relative
  • Brain chemistry: Imbalances in neurotransmitters
  • Hormonal imbalances: May trigger or worsen condition

Prevention

  • Early intervention at first signs
  • Avoiding drugs and alcohol
  • Stress management techniques
  • Regular sleep schedule
  • Mood monitoring

Treatment

  • Medications: Mood stabilizers, antipsychotics, antidepressants
  • Psychotherapy: CBT, family-focused therapy, psychoeducation
  • Lifestyle changes: Regular exercise, healthy diet, sleep routine
  • ECT: For severe cases unresponsive to medication

Self-care

  • Educate yourself about the condition
  • Stick to treatment plan
  • Monitor moods and symptoms
  • Build healthy routines
  • Join a support group
  • Practice stress-reduction techniques

Helping Someone

  • Learn about bipolar disorder
  • Encourage treatment adherence
  • Be patient
  • Listen without judgment
  • Watch for warning signs of mood shifts
  • Take suicidal talk seriously
  • Take care of yourself too
